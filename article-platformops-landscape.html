PlatformOps - Understanding your landscape (Theme Park) tool

For platform engineers and architects

The Theme Park Analogy for Platform Engineering

Understanding your platform landscape becomes clearer when you think of it like a theme park. This tool helps you map out the different areas, attractions, and infrastructure that make up your platform ecosystem.

Theme Park Components

Map your platform using these theme park elements:

  • Entrance Gates: Authentication and authorization systems
  • Main Street: Developer portal and onboarding experience
  • Attractions: Core platform services and capabilities
  • Infrastructure: Underlying compute, storage, and networking
  • Guest Services: Support, documentation, and help desk
  • Transportation: CI/CD pipelines and deployment mechanisms

Mapping Exercise

Use this framework to assess your current landscape:

  • Visitor Journey: Map the developer experience from onboarding to production
  • Attraction Popularity: Which services are most/least used?
  • Wait Times: Where are the bottlenecks and delays?
  • Maintenance Needs: What requires the most operational overhead?
  • Guest Satisfaction: How do developers rate each service?

PlatformOps Principles

Apply these operational principles to your platform:

  • Customer Experience: Developers are your customers
  • Self-Service: Enable developers to help themselves
  • Observability: Monitor both technical metrics and user experience
  • Continuous Improvement: Regular feedback loops and iterations
  • Scalability: Design for growth and changing needs

Workshop Activities

Run these activities with your team:

  • Landscape Mapping (15 min): Draw your current platform as a theme park
  • Pain Point Identification (10 min): Mark areas with long "wait times"
  • User Journey Analysis (15 min): Walk through a typical developer's day
  • Improvement Prioritization (5 min): Identify top 3 areas for improvement

Expected Outcomes

This exercise will help you:

  • Visualize your platform from a user perspective
  • Identify operational bottlenecks and pain points
  • Prioritize platform improvements based on user impact
  • Align team understanding of the current state
  • Create a shared language for discussing platform operations
