Team Topologies

For team leads and organizational designers

Organizing Teams for Platform Engineering Success

Team Topologies provides a framework for organizing software teams to optimize for fast flow and reduced cognitive load. This approach is essential for successful platform engineering implementations.

The Four Team Types

Team Topologies defines four fundamental team types:

  • Stream-aligned teams: Focus on a single valuable stream of work (feature teams)
  • Platform teams: Provide self-service capabilities to stream-aligned teams
  • Enabling teams: Help other teams overcome obstacles and adopt new technologies
  • Complicated-subsystem teams: Focus on areas requiring specialized knowledge

Platform Team Characteristics

Effective platform teams in this model:

  • Treat the platform as a product with stream-aligned teams as customers
  • Focus on reducing cognitive load for application teams
  • Provide self-service capabilities and clear boundaries
  • Measure success by the productivity of their users
  • Maintain strong feedback loops with consuming teams

Team Interaction Modes

Teams interact in three primary ways:

  • Collaboration: Working closely together for discovery and rapid learning
  • X-as-a-Service: Consuming or providing something with minimal collaboration
  • Facilitating: Helping another team to clear impediments

Platform Engineering Applications

Apply Team Topologies to your platform initiative:

  • Structure your platform team as a product team serving internal customers
  • Use enabling teams to help with platform adoption and onboarding
  • Keep stream-aligned teams focused on business value delivery
  • Design clear interfaces between platform and application teams
  • Regularly assess and evolve team interactions based on organizational needs

Cognitive Load Management

Reduce cognitive load by:

  • Providing simple, well-documented platform interfaces
  • Abstracting infrastructure complexity from application teams
  • Creating consistent patterns and standards
  • Offering self-service capabilities with guardrails
  • Minimizing the number of tools and technologies teams need to learn

Team Sizing and Conway's Law

Consider these organizational factors:

  • Keep teams small (typically 5-9 people) for effective communication
  • Align team boundaries with desired architecture
  • Design team interactions to support your platform goals
  • Plan for team evolution as the platform matures

Implementation Steps

Getting started with Team Topologies:

  • Map your current teams and their interactions
  • Identify which teams fit the four fundamental types
  • Assess cognitive load on each team
  • Design target team topology aligned with platform goals
  • Plan transition steps and success metrics
  • Regularly review and adjust team structures
