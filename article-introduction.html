Introduction to platform engineering

For: Anyone interested

Platform Engineering: A Friendly Introduction

Think of a Theme Park — full of rides, attractions, and services, all interconnected and dependent on one another. For the park to thrive, everything must run smoothly: rides need to be safe and reliable, staff coordinated, the experience seamless, and the whole environment kept secure, up to date, and compliant. Behind the scenes, it’s a complex, layered system.

Organisations are no different. Regardless of industry, size, or domain, they are also complex ecosystems. Their success depends on platforms: the shared foundations that curate and deliver common capabilities. Just as rides in a theme park rely on standard infrastructure and services, platforms provide technology teams with reusable building blocks.

Each platform is a system of people, processes, and technology working together to deliver value through services. Platforms can exist at different levels of abstraction — from infrastructure to developer enablement — and often share dependencies. Platform Engineering is the discipline that makes these capabilities reliable and consumable.

Rooted in DevOps principles, Platform Engineering focuses on reducing complexity, standardising what can be standardised, and minimising errors — all to accelerate delivery and improve consistency. It is a key ingredient in enabling cohesiveness, scalability, speed, and governance at scale.

Returning to the theme park analogy: Platform Engineering ensures each ride, show, or attraction can focus on its unique experience, while consuming standardised components as a service — available on demand, self-served, and reliable.

The mechanism through which these services are offered is often called an Internal Developer Platform (IDP), and the way they are consumed follows the familiar “X-as-a-Service” (XaaS) model.

What Does Platform Engineering Need?

Technology and tooling are important, but success depends just as much — if not more — on capabilities and ways of working. At a high level, it relies on:

  • Clear vision and objectives to guide the platform’s purpose.
  • Investment in both people and technology.
  • Engineering expertise to support modern practices.
  • A product mindset so the platform continually evolves as a product.
  • A service mindset so platform services are reliable and well-managed.
  • Adoption focus, ensuring the people who use the platform can succeed with it.
  • Platform teams with the right structure, flow-oriented ways of working, and mindset.
