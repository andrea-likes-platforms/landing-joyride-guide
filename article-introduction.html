Introduction to platform engineering

For: Developers, Engineering Managers

What is Platform Engineering?

Platform engineering is about creating a product-like foundation that enables teams to deliver software faster, safer, and with less friction.
Instead of every team reinventing the wheel, platform engineering provides the right tools, practices, and golden paths — so developers can focus on business value, not plumbing.

While a lot can be found on platform engineering from the perspectives of developers or technical roles, here are introductions for non-technical key roles inside a Platform Engineering introduction.

For middle management (ART leadership, Agile Coaches, Change Managers)

  • Platform Engineering provides a practical way to reduce dependencies and bottlenecks in your organization that often arise around shared services.
  • It helps reducing cognitive load on development teams, enables faster time-to-market and improves security and compliance

Key Components

A successful platform typically includes:

  • Developer Portal: Central hub for documentation and services
  • Self-Service Infrastructure: Automated provisioning and management
  • CI/CD Pipelines: Standardized deployment workflows
  • Observability: Monitoring and logging capabilities

Getting Started

Begin your platform engineering journey by understanding your organization's specific needs and pain points. Focus on solving real problems rather than building technology for its own sake.
