Securing Executive Buy-in for Platform Engineering

Getting investment and sponsorship for platform engineering initiatives requires a strategic approach that speaks to business outcomes and organizational pain points.

Understanding the Business Case

Before approaching stakeholders, you need to clearly articulate:

  • Current development bottlenecks and their business impact
  • Time-to-market delays and opportunity costs
  • Developer productivity metrics and trends
  • Technical debt and maintenance overhead

Building Your Investment Proposal

Structure your proposal around these key elements:

  • Problem Statement: Quantify current inefficiencies
  • Solution Overview: High-level platform engineering approach
  • Expected Outcomes: Measurable improvements in delivery speed and quality
  • Investment Requirements: Team, tools, and timeline
  • Risk Mitigation: How you'll handle common challenges

Identifying Key Stakeholders

Map out who needs to be involved:

  • Engineering leadership and CTOs
  • Product management leaders
  • Business unit heads affected by delivery speed
  • Finance and budgeting teams
  • HR for team scaling considerations

Presenting Your Case

When presenting to executives:

  • Start with business impact, not technical details
  • Use data and metrics wherever possible
  • Show examples from similar organizations
  • Be realistic about timelines and challenges
  • Propose a phased approach with clear milestones
