Platforms as Systems of Value

The evidence is clear.

Organisations that fail to evolve their internal platforms risk slower responses to change, rising operational costs, and a widening gap between technology investment and realised business value.

The organisations that are pulling ahead are doing something different; they ensure that platforms and their teams have what they need in order to succeed. They recognise that platforms organised and managed well become engines of resilience, speed, and innovation across the business. Left unmanaged, they become costly stacks that fail to deliver a return on investment.

In that sense, this paper is a case for Platform Engineering as an undoubtedly key piece of the puzzle, with emphasis on describing not what Platform Engineering is, but:

  • Why Platform Engineering is key
  • What it needs to succeed
  • Evidence and industry insights supporting this position

A position paper by Gielen Rojas-Lopez exploring the dimensions that should be considered for platform evolution and offering guidance on how and where to start across those dimensions.

Use it when you want to:

  • Understand the key dimensions of the overall operating model that are essential for Platform Engineering to succeed.
  • Plan the areas to assess and identify stakeholders to involve, using the dimensions as guidance.
  • Start planning and outline likely activities using the Evolving Platforms guidance.
