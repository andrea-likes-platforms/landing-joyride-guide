Architecture for Flow

For: System Architects, Platform Engineers

Overview

Architecture for Flow provides guidance in designing adaptive, socio-technical systems. It combines three powerful methodologies that complement each other in a structured way to create systems that enable smooth, efficient workflows across your organization.

Key Components

Architecture for Flow integrates three essential tools:

  • Wardley Mapping: A strategic tool for understanding your landscape and evolution patterns
  • Domain Driven Design: A modeling approach for tackling complexity in the heart of software
  • Team Topologies: Organizational patterns for effective team structures and interactions

Why Combine These Approaches?

Each methodology addresses different aspects of system design:

  • Wardley Mapping helps you understand where you are and where you need to go
  • Domain Driven Design helps you model what you're building
  • Team Topologies helps you organize how you're building it

Benefits

By combining these methodologies in a structured way, Architecture for Flow helps organizations create systems that are:

  • Technically sound and evolutionarily aligned
  • Organizationally coherent with clear team boundaries
  • Adaptive to changing business needs
  • Optimized for fast flow of value from conception to delivery
