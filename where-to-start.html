Getting Started with Platform Engineering

Starting your platform engineering journey can feel overwhelming, but with the right approach, you can build momentum quickly and deliver value to your organization.

Step 1: Assess Your Current State

Before building anything new, understand where you are today:

  • Map out your current development workflow
  • Identify pain points and bottlenecks
  • Survey your development teams
  • Document existing tools and processes

Step 2: Define Your Platform Vision

Create a clear vision for what success looks like:

  • Establish measurable goals (deployment frequency, lead time, etc.)
  • Define your target developer experience
  • Align with business objectives
  • Set realistic timelines and milestones

Step 3: Start Small with Quick Wins

Begin with high-impact, low-effort improvements:

  • Standardize development environments
  • Implement basic CI/CD pipelines
  • Create self-service documentation
  • Automate common tasks

Step 4: Build Your Platform Team

Assemble the right mix of skills and experience:

  • Platform engineers with infrastructure expertise
  • Developer experience specialists
  • DevOps and automation engineers
  • Product managers who understand developer needs

Step 5: Iterate and Expand

Continuously improve based on feedback:

  • Collect regular feedback from development teams
  • Measure and track key metrics
  • Expand platform capabilities gradually
  • Foster a culture of continuous improvement
