For platform leaders and product managers

Crafting Your Platform Engineering Vision

A clear vision and problem statement forms the foundation of successful platform engineering initiatives. This framework helps you articulate what you're building and why.

Example Vision Framework

Vision: "We will create a self-service platform that enables our development teams to deploy secure, scalable applications in minutes, not days, while maintaining enterprise-grade reliability and compliance."

Problem Statement: "Currently, our developers spend 40% of their time on operational tasks instead of building features. It takes an average of 3 days to provision new environments and 2 weeks to deploy to production, limiting our ability to respond to market opportunities."

The Vision Statement Framework

Your platform vision should answer these key questions:

  • What: What kind of platform are you building?
  • Who: Who are your primary users and stakeholders?
  • Why: What business outcomes will this enable?
  • How: What's your approach to achieving this vision?

Problem Definition Template

Structure your problem statement using this template:

  • Current State: Describe how things work today
  • Pain Points: Identify specific problems and their impact
  • Root Causes: What's causing these problems?
  • Desired Future State: What would success look like?

Stakeholder Alignment

Ensure your vision resonates with different audiences:

  • Developers: Focus on improved experience and productivity
  • Business Leaders: Emphasize speed to market and innovation
  • Operations: Highlight reliability and efficiency gains
  • Security: Address compliance and risk reduction

Measuring Success

Define measurable outcomes for your vision:

  • Developer productivity metrics (deployment frequency, lead time)
  • Business impact indicators (time to market, feature velocity)
  • Operational efficiency measures (uptime, incident resolution)
  • User satisfaction scores and feedback

Vision Communication

Make your vision actionable and memorable:

  • Keep it concise (elevator pitch length)
  • Use concrete examples and scenarios
  • Create visual representations or diagrams
  • Tell stories about the developer experience
  • Regularly revisit and refine based on feedback
